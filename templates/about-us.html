About PIH at Berkeley

At Partners in Health, our mission is to provide a preferential option for the poor in health care.

PIH at UC Berkeley aims to use some of the most valuable tools in social movement formation to create a group of like-minded, passionate individuals fighting for the human right to health. The tools we will be using include but are not limited to: education, advocacy, and campaign planning. We are looking for creative minds from a plethora of academic and cultural backgrounds in order to allow a diversity of voices to be heard regarding health care disparities and social justice on a domestic as well as international level.

Our Goals

We aspire to achieve 3 overarching goals in one year. In order to achieve these goals we will have a DeCal, advocacy activities, fundraising activities, the leadership team will be involved in a letter to the editor writing campaign, and we will be connecting with local congressmen (some of whom have already established connections with PIH while others will need to be forged w/ the tools we will be providing). Very briefly, our three goals are as follows:

Generate

In the past PIH | Engage chapters have raised up to $1,000,000 as a result of their advocacy efforts and activities -- the same results are expected from the PIH | Engage at UC Berkeley chapter. Information regarding where this money goes can be found here: http://www.pih.org/our-work

Advocate

We will have meetings with congressmen, LTE writing campaigns, and events such as the "Social for Justice" and the "Global Day of Action" among others, in order to advocate for the human right to health and for equitable heialth care delivery globally and locally.

Educate

We aspire to use literacy tools in order to instigate conversation regarding the relevance of health care and its equitable delivery. We will eventually be creating a "Global Health Guide" which will be a compilation of lectures, excerpts from readings as well as the voices of UC Berkeley students and Berkeley city members regarding healthcare disparities and the quickly transforming field of Global Health.
